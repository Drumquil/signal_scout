## 1. Purpose

These are first-pass permission and outreach drafts for the current priority sources in the outreach queue.

They are intentionally plain and direct. Each draft:

- describes Signal Scout accurately;
- makes clear that Signal Scout is a buyer-discovery tool, not a replacement marketplace;
- asks for permission to ingest factual metadata only;
- offers attribution and deep-linking back to source;
- leaves room for the recipient to suggest a better sanctioned path.

## 2. Shared Positioning Block

Use this positioning consistently in outreach:

Signal Scout is a buyer-side cattle discovery and alerting tool being developed by Drumquil Signal. It helps subscribed buyers find relevant cattle opportunities more quickly by matching listing facts against buyer criteria and then directing the buyer back to the original source or agent to enquire, bid, or transact.

Signal Scout is not intended to replace the source marketplace or republish full source content. The working model is facts-only ingestion with attribution and link-back.

## 3. Draft 01 - The Herd Online

**To:** `admin@theherdonline.com.au`  
**Subject:** Permission request for Signal Scout buyer alerts using The Herd listing facts

Hello Sam / The Herd team,

I am building Signal Scout, a Drumquil Signal product for cattle buyers. It is a buyer-side discovery and alerting tool that matches subscribed buyers to relevant cattle opportunities and then directs them back to the original source to enquire or transact.

I am writing because The Herd is one of the most relevant livestock information sources in Australia for saleyard and paddock-sale visibility, and I wanted to ask directly about a sanctioned way for Signal Scout to work with your listings.

The use case I have in mind is narrow:

- ingest factual listing metadata only from public listings or sale notices;
- no login-wall access;
- no copying of photos or full listing text unless separately approved;
- every alert would attribute The Herd and link the buyer back to the original listing or source page.

I understand your current terms restrict scraping and automated collection, so I am not looking to proceed without your approval. If there is a better path, I would be very open to that as well, whether that is:

- written permission for limited public-page ingestion;
- a feed or structured export;
- or a broader partnership/referral arrangement.

If helpful, I can send a one-page summary of how Signal Scout works and exactly what fields would be stored and shown.

Kind regards,

Tom Flanagan  
Drumquil Signal  
tom@drumquilsignal.com
