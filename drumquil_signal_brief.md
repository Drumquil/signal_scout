# Drumquil Signal Brief

Marketing-relevant distillation for brand, website, and early go-to-market work.
This is not a technical source of truth.

## Core Position

Drumquil Signal builds buyer-side market intelligence tools for Australian cattle
markets.

The company helps cattle buyers find relevant opportunities sooner, understand
those opportunities more clearly, and spend less time manually checking scattered
sale channels.

The first product is Signal Scout. The broader direction is a suite of practical
tools that combine listing discovery, buyer criteria, alerting, and independent
market signals.

## Product Story

### Signal Scout

Signal Scout monitors online cattle listings, filters lots against a buyer's own
criteria, and sends WhatsApp alerts when a matching opportunity appears.

It is built for restockers and livestock buyers who know what they want but do
not have time to keep refreshing every platform, catalogue, agent page, or sale
notice.

Current beta focus:

- Australian cattle sale listings.
- AuctionsPlus as the first active source.
- Buyer criteria captured through a simple intake form.
- WhatsApp alerts when relevant mobs appear.
- Reliability, onboarding, filtering, and alert quality before expansion.

Future direction:

- More online sources.
- Where feasible, structured or semi-structured physical saleyard information.
- Better buyer workflows around watchlists, replies, and feedback.
- Integration with fair value signals from Signal Model.

### Signal Model

Signal Model is the planned companion price intelligence product.

It will provide fair value estimates and market signals for cattle categories,
with confidence levels. It should be framed as decision support, not advice.

Use language like:

- "fair value estimate"
- "market signal"
- "confidence"
- "current conditions"

Avoid language like:

- "buy this"
- "sell now"
- "guaranteed return"
- "profit alert"

Signal Model is deferred until Signal Scout beta stabilisation and required
backtesting are complete.

## Strategic Difference

Most existing tools help users browse, list, calculate, or read market reports.
Drumquil Signal should stand for something sharper:

Relevant livestock opportunities, matched to the buyer, delivered before the
sale, with an independent view of market value layered in over time.

The practical promise is not "more data". It is less noise, earlier notice, and
better-prepared buying decisions.

## Primary Customer Archetype

The first customer is a Northern Rivers or NSW cattle restocker who:

- Buys cattle opportunistically but with clear preferences.
- Watches price, season, weight, breed, class, location, and mob size closely.
- Has limited time for manual online browsing.
- Relies on trusted relationships and practical judgement.
- Is open to useful technology but sceptical of shiny software.
- Wants alerts that save time, not another dashboard to manage.

They are likely busy, commercially minded, and allergic to vague claims. They
will value the product if it helps them inspect, bid on, or ignore opportunities
faster than their current routine.

Secondary audiences:

- Extension and advisory networks who can refer credible beta users.
- Agents and saleyards, later, if buyer demand can be proven.
- Platform or data partners, later, once beta traction exists.

## Customer Problems

The website and marketing should speak to these problems:

- Relevant cattle are spread across platforms, agents, notices, and catalogues.
- Buyers miss opportunities because they do not see them early enough.
- Manual browsing is repetitive and easy to neglect.
- Listings contain mixed levels of detail, so filtering by hand takes judgement.
- Buyers want confidence on whether a lot fits their program before sale day.
- Fair value context is hard to assemble quickly from market, seasonal, and local
  signals.

## Brand Voice

Drumquil Signal should sound:

- Clear.
- Grounded.
- Commercially practical.
- Rural-literate.
- Calmly analytical.
- Human and direct.
- Useful before it is impressive.

It should avoid:

- Hype.
- Overly polished startup language.
- Generic "AI-powered" claims.
- Big promises before beta evidence exists.
- Talking down to producers.
- Treating agriculture as a lifestyle aesthetic.

The voice should feel like a capable market analyst sitting beside a working
cattle buyer: concise, practical, and respectful of local knowledge.

## Website Job

The first website does not need to sell a mature software suite. It needs to
create trust, explain the product clearly, and recruit the right beta users.

Primary goals:

- Establish Drumquil Signal as the parent brand.
- Explain Signal Scout in plain language.
- Make the beta feel credible, limited, and practical.
- Encourage qualified restockers to express interest or talk to Tom.
- Set expectations that Scout is an alerting tool, not a replacement for buyer
  judgement.
- Leave room for Signal Model and future tools without pretending they are live.

Near-term calls to action:

- Join the Signal Scout beta.
- Request a conversation.
- Submit buyer criteria.
- Get notified when paid access opens.

Trust signals to include when available:

- Australian cattle-market focus.
- ABN and business contact details.
- Clear privacy and opt-in language before paid launch.
- Plain explanation of WhatsApp alerts.
- Beta status and limited-user rollout.
- Evidence from beta once collected: alerts sent, users onboarded, useful lots
  surfaced, attendance or bidding outcomes.

## Website Structure

Recommended early site structure:

- Home: Drumquil Signal, buyer-side cattle market intelligence.
- Signal Scout: what it does, who it is for, how beta works.
- Signal Model: coming later, fair value signals and confidence framing.
- About: why the company exists and who is building it.
- Beta: eligibility, expectations, and intake.
- Contact: direct route to Tom.

Do not lead with a generic software landing page. Lead with the problem:
Australian cattle buyers miss relevant opportunities because the market is
fragmented and time-poor.

## Copy Guardrails

Use current-state claims carefully:

- Say Signal Scout is in beta.
- Say AuctionsPlus is the first active source only if platform references are
  appropriate in context.
- Say multi-source monitoring is the direction, not current reality.
- Say Signal Model is planned or in development, not live.
- Say outputs support decisions, not that they make decisions.

Avoid:

- Paid-user claims before legal gates are complete.
- Platform partnership implications unless consent exists.
- "All listings" or "never miss a lot" language.
- Buy/sell recommendations.
- Any claim that depends on unbuilt source integrations.

## Visual Direction

The brand should feel modern, precise, and agricultural without becoming rustic
or decorative.

Useful visual cues:

- Real Australian cattle, saleyard, paddock, or catalogue context.
- Clean information design.
- Strong typography.
- Restrained colour palette with one confident signal colour.
- Visual language that suggests clarity, filtering, and timely alerts.

Avoid:

- Generic tech gradients.
- Cartoon livestock.
- Overly glossy SaaS imagery.
- Stock photos that feel disconnected from Australian cattle buying.
- A logo that only says "farm" without saying "market intelligence".

## Marketing Strategy Implications

Near-term marketing should be relationship-led, not ad-led.

The best first channel is a small group of known or referred restockers through
Tom's network, including DPIRD or extension-adjacent contacts. The beta should
produce learning, trust, and proof before broad promotion.

Useful beta questions:

- Did the alert surface a lot the buyer would otherwise have missed?
- Did it save time?
- Did it influence attendance, inspection, bidding, or purchase behaviour?
- Was the alert too broad, too narrow, too early, or too late?
- Would the buyer pay for it after a time-limited beta?

Marketing should evolve from these proof points, not from assumed benefits.

## One-Line Positioning Options

- Buyer-side cattle market intelligence for Australian restockers.
- Cattle sale alerts matched to the way you actually buy.
- Less catalogue noise. Earlier notice. Better-prepared cattle buying.
- Practical market signals for time-poor cattle buyers.
